Periyar National Park and Wildlife Sanctuary



About Periyar National Park and Wildlife Sanctuary:
  1. One of South India's most popular tourist attractions
  2. Periyar National Park and Wildlife Sanctuary is centered around a lake built by British engineers in 1895 for irrigation and to provide water to the city of Madurai.
  3. Established in 1934, this beautiful park is home to numerous species of mammals, including a large free-roaming Indian elephant population, wild boar, otters, the lion-tailed macaque, and more than 20 Bengal tigers.
  4. Bird watching is a popular activity with frequent sightings of species such as darters, storks, kingfishers, hornbills, and racket-tailed drongos, along with many interesting varieties of butterflies.
What to do in the Sanctuary:
  1. The best ways to enjoy the park's splendid mountain scenery are to take a lake cruise or guided jungle walk
  2. The latter allowing visitors a chance to come face to face with elephant herds and observe other wildlife from watchtowers and viewing platforms.
Hot Tip:
  • Be sure to stop at one of the many nearby spice, tea, or coffee plantations for a tour.
